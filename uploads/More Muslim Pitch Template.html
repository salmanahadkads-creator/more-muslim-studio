The Show

What is More Muslim?

A narrative audio documentary series that explores the Muslim experience, with all its messiness.

Each episode is a transhistorical journey into one aspect of the Muslim experience, reported across continents, edited from Doha, and powered by the Al-Mujadilah Center and Mosque for Women.

The Show

One of the first narrative documentary podcasts made in Qatar.

With the show, we're bringing the quality and calibre of storytelling audiences love from public radio shows like Radiolab, Planet Money, and This American Life to tell stories from Muslim communities around the world.

Editorially led from Doha, we work with reporting from correspondents across multiple continents, already competing in the same category as the world's leading narrative podcasts.

Why This, Why Now

Prestige narrative audio is the next frontier for podcasts, and no one is making it from here.

01

The podcasts market has become saturated with chat shows in recent years, especially in the Middle East, but few in the entire region are making hand-crafted, cinematic, expertly edited and sound designed stories the way we are.

02

We focus on telling stories from Muslim communities around the world in a way that doesn't oversimplify or stereotype, but instead provide a shared ground for people to understand what being Muslim is really like.

03

We have put together a team of journalists that is truly international and have worked in some of the biggest news organizations in the world.

What These Stories Are About

A Muslim lens on universal questions.

More Muslim is documentary journalism that appeals to a much broader audience beyond Muslims. The subjects are the ones every modern listener is already thinking about. Technology and mental health, memory and belonging, home and displacement, heard through the lives of Muslims around the world.

Episode 06

Cape Malay

Growing up, Aina had heard about the transatlantic slave trade. But on a reporting trip she was shocked to learn that Dutch colonisers were also deporting and enslaving Muslims from Indonesia, shipping them thousands of miles to South Africa. Reporter Aina J. Khan takes us to Cape Town and tells the story of what they built there.

How the Cape Malay used their faith to survive 400 years of slavery, colonialism, and apartheid. And why gentrification may be what finally breaks them.

Episode 07

In Therapy, With SheikhAGPT

When reporter Yassmin Abdel-Magied's friend tells her she's been using ChatGPT as a therapist, Yassmin doesn't know what to think. The chatbot calls her friend "habibti." Gives her Islamic relationship advice. It's helping her reconnect with her faith in ways no human in her life has been able to. But it's also a product built by a tech company with no foundations in Islamic psychology.

Who's Listening

The curious, mobile, globally-minded listener.

More Muslim's audience skews 22 to 45, university-educated, urban and culturally engaged. The same person who reads long-form, travels often, and chooses Radiolab over the algorithm. They listen on the move: commuting, in transit, in the air.

It is a young, international, predominantly female audience that crosses cultures rather than staying inside one. Reached across 143 countries inside the first three months.

"The kind of passenger who often flies through Doha on a layover."
