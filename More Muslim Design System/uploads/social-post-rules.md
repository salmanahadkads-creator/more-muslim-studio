## 12. Social Post Rules

These rules apply to all branded Instagram and social-media posts (1080×1080, 1080×1350, 1080×1920). They override or refine the general brand guidelines where social-specific behaviour differs.

### Typography

- **No muted text.** All type runs at full opacity — never apply `opacity` less than 1 to text elements on a social post. The brand does not whisper.
- **No italic.** Social posts use upright Roman only. Italic is reserved for pull-quotes inside long-form contexts (pitch decks, editorial pages), never social.
- **Caps tracking is universal.** Every uppercase string — eyebrows, CTAs, URLs, labels — uses `letter-spacing: 0.165em`. No exceptions, no retracking for decorative variation.
- **Scale up, not down.** Minimum sizes for social:
  - Display headline: `96px` minimum
  - Eyebrow / CTA labels: `48px`
  - Body copy: `32px`
- **Body line-height is 1.2.** All body copy on social posts uses `line-height: 1.2`. Do not use looser values (1.4, 1.5) — they waste vertical space at social scale.
- **Text alignment is centred.** All elements align to the horizontal centre. No left-aligned social posts.
- **Text-wrap is `balance`.** Use `text-wrap: balance` on body paragraphs so multi-line copy breaks symmetrically.

### Layout

- **Distribute, don't cluster.** Elements should be spaced evenly across the full canvas height (`justify-content: space-around` or explicit even gaps). Do not group everything into a centre block with large dead zones at top and bottom.
- **No decorative separators.** No horizontal rules, dashes, or line dividers between text elements. Whitespace alone creates separation.
- **No padding-heavy containers.** The canvas is the frame. Avoid large uniform padding blocks that shrink the effective live area — let `space-around` or explicit per-element spacing do the work.

### Brand marks

- **No horizontal logo lockup on social posts.** The lockup (symbol + wordmark) belongs to pitch decks, website headers, and partner materials. On social, the symbol alone is the sign-off.
- **Symbol is the anchor.** The brand symbol sits at the bottom centre as the sole brand identifier. Size it prominently — minimum `140px` on a 1080×1350 canvas, scaled proportionally for other formats.
- **Symbol at full opacity.** Do not dim the symbol — it runs at full `color` value, no `opacity` reduction.

### Copy

- **Never use the show tagline as body copy.** "A narrative audio documentary series that explores the Muslim experience, with all its messiness" is a show description for pitch and press contexts. Body copy on a social post must be specific to the subject of that post.
- **Be concrete and specific.** Tell the reader exactly what the post is about. Abstract brand language is a placeholder, not content. If the post announces a job, describe the job. If it announces an episode, describe the episode.
